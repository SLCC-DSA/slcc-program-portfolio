Setting the Standard

Establishing SLCC's Living Wage "Waterline" for Program Classification

What is a Family-Sustaining Wage?

As part of the Aspen Unlocking Opportunity initiative, our primary goal is to ensure that SLCC's workforce programs lead students to credentials of value. We define "value" as the ability to earn a wage that not only covers basic needs but also allows for savings, advancement, and economic mobility for a family. This is our "waterline" for program success.

Setting Our Waterline: Why $39.00/hr?

Local research (EPI/MIT) indicates a true living wage for one adult and one child in Salt Lake County is $86,000/yr ($41.65/hr). However, national research from NCII defines a "Good Job" as topping out around $72,000/yr ($34.61/hr). We set our waterline at $81,120/yr ($39.00/hr) as an ambitious and data-informed compromise, bridging the gap between a "Good Job" and the full living wage standard.

Beyond Wages: Other Key Factors

Student Demographics

We must consider the typical family size of our students. Since a high proportion are parents, our wage threshold is based on a family unit (1 adult, 1 child), not just a single individual.

Job Demand & Growth

A high wage is only valuable if jobs are available. We analyze the 5-Year Job Change and local LMI data to ensure programs lead to high-opportunity and high-demand careers.

Transfer & Articulation

For transfer programs, value is measured differently. We analyze 1-year transfer rates and the number of articulation agreements to ensure seamless pathways to a bachelor's degree.

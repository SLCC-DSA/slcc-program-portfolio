Our Evolving Mission: From Completion to Value

Our strategic focus is shifting. Access (Community College 1.0) and Completion (2.0) are no longer enough. We are embracing Community College 3.0, a new benchmark focused on Post-Completion Value.

CC 1.0: ACCESS

Opening the doors of higher education to all.

CC 2.0: COMPLETION

Improving persistence and graduation rates.

CC 3.0: VALUE

Ensuring credentials lead to high-ROI careers and seamless transfer.

Our "Why": Mission & The "Waterline"

Our Unique Student Body

Our diverse population often enrolls part-time, balancing work and family. Therefore, we must measure "value" against a realistic, family-sustaining wage.

The Three Drivers of Our Unified Strategy

THE CATALYST: HB 265

"Why Now?"

This state mandate provided the impetus for change and strategic reinvestment. It forced a needed review and gave us the evaluation framework to move forward.

THE ENGINE: Aspen

"How?"

Aspen provides the national best-practice methodology. It gives us the data-driven framework to define value, classify our programs, and identify proven strategies.

THE MISSION: Vision Matrix

"Why Us?"

This is our institutional North Star. It ensures the student experience is clear and accessible, fulfilling our "Thrive" pillar by focusing on student ROI.

Driver 1: The Legacy of HB 265

What It Was (The Past)

  • A state mandate to reallocate funds from low-value to high-demand programs.
  • Resulted in the discontinuation of ~40 programs (Summer 2025).
  • Initiated a necessary, though difficult, change management process.

What It Is Now (The Legacy)

  • The name is being left behind to move past the trauma.
  • The process gave us a robust Program Evaluation Framework.
  • This framework is being adopted as our new, continuous Iterative Program Review Cycle.
  • This replaces the slow, 7-year UBHE R411 review cycle.

Driver 1: Our New Iterative Program Review Rubric

We are adopting the strong metrics from the HB 265 process, finalizing them by Jan 2026. This rubric forms the basis of our new continuous improvement cycle, managed by Deans and Associate Deans.

1. Enrollment Data

Demonstrated student enrollment.

2. Completion Rates

Graduation and timely completion.

3. Post-Completion Outcomes

Wages, employment, and licensure.

4. Program Demands

Current/future local & state market.

5. Program Cost

Cost of delivering the program.

6. Institutional Mission

Alignment with SLCC's mission/role.

Driver 2: The Engine (Aspen Unlocking Opportunity)

Joined in Fall 2025, Aspen provides the outward-looking methodology to analyze our 208 active programs and classify them based on real-world value.

1. Labor Force Assessment

  • Analyzed socio-economic factors in Salt Lake County.
  • Established the $86,000 "waterline" (living wage).
  • Mapped programs to occupations, hourly wages, and future demand.

2. Partner Engagement Assessment

  • Analyzed transfer rates of SLCC graduates.
  • Counted active, high-quality articulation agreements for each program.
  • Set a binary classification for transfer success.

Driver 3: The Mission (Vision Matrix Team 2)

This is the "student-facing" part of the strategy. Vision Matrix "closes the loop" by ensuring the high-value pathways we've defined are experienced by students with...

Our Path Forward: A Continuous Cycle

Data-Driven Strategies for Program Improvement

This classification is not a one-time event. It is the foundation for a new, iterative program review cycle. Our actions will be transparent and strategic, with guidance from Aspen.

FOR HIGH-VALUE

EXPAND

Target resources to increase enrollment and close equity gaps for underserved students.

FOR MID-VALUE

STRENGTHEN

Invest in curriculum, employer partnerships, and articulation agreements to move them to high-value.

FOR LOW-VALUE

REVIEW

Implement performance improvement plans or make data-driven decisions to sunset and reinvest.

Implementation Timeline

  • Spring 2025: HB 265 process began.
  • Summer 2025: Reallocation completed (~40 programs discontinued).
  • Fall 2025: Joined Aspen. Established waterline & classification rubric. Analyzed 208 programs.
  • Nov 2025: Deans & Associate Deans review classifications and provide feedback.
  • Dec 2025: Submit enrollment data & classifications to Aspen Institute for review.
  • Jan 2026: Finalize and adopt the new Iterative Program Review Rubric.
  • Spring 2026: Apply new rubric to classified programs. Analyze full dataset. Begin developing strategies (Expand, Strengthen, Review).
  • June 2026: Vision Matrix implementation phases out, handing off to this new sustainable, iterative cycle.
